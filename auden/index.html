Wystan Hugh Auden

Yorkshire, England.
English Poet

Reputation

WH Auden was renowned across Britain during the Great Depression
His popularity grew as he wrote plays alongside Christopher Isherwood, both were homosexual. This was not uncommon among young writers, however. Feelings of isolation and being forced to lead a double life sparked the creativity of many modern poets. The Modern era was the perfect opportunity for these bold young authors to freely express pain, anxiety, and longing from hiding who they were.

Inspiration

After moving to Berlin in college, Auden grew to love the German language.
Auden adopted a love of reading at a young age from poets such as William Blake, Emily Dickinson, Robert Frost, Thomas Hardy, and Gerard Manley Hopkins.

Legacy

Auden's prose and poetry inspired writers such as
• C. Day Lewis who was named poet laureate in 1968
• Louis MacNeice
• Stephen Spender who printed the first collection of Auden’s poems in 1928 by hand.
